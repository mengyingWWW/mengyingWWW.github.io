<h2>Why Natural Material System</h2>

Architecture can learn from natural material systems due to their generative, computational, and responsive qualities. Natural systems have inherent geometry and structure (micro-biomorphism) and showcase self-organization when influenced by energy distribution changes (natural computational automata), forming integrated systems. By adopting these principles, architecture can create spaces that are reactive to their environment.

<h2>Natural Computational System of Brain Coral</h2>

This study extracts architectural inspiration from the multi-scale structure of brain coral. At the macro scale, differential growth patterns respond to environmental energy variations, informing site-responsive form distribution. At the meso scale, branch-like internal growth logic suggests spatial organization and structural connectivity strategies. At the micro scale, porous skeletal geometries reveal surface articulation and material performance potentials. Together, these layered observations translate biological growth mechanisms into generative architectural design principles.
